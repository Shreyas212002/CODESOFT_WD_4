Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

About Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Dr. Kalam was a scientist and aeronautical engineer who worked with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). He played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997.

Dr. Kalam died on July 27, 2015, at the age of 83. He is remembered for his contributions to science and technology, and for his inspiring words and vision for the future of India.
